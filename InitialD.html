Initial D Techniques/Theories

Ryosuke's FC3S vs Rin's R32 (Fifth Stage)

- FC3S climb slope like a bank after being pushed out of line by R32 to avoid hitting curb

- FC3S's high speed cornering entry releases side G-Force to absorb impact from side press by R32

- FC3S braking while being side pressed by R32 to allow R32 to slide forward and understeer towards the railing, and using oversteer to cut the line of R32

Keisuke's FD3S vs Go's NSX (Fifth Stage)

- Uphill battles are usually turbo vs turbo, with exception to NSX due to its large displacement NA engine mounted in the center of chassis

- This allows NSX to have good cornering speed due to mid engine and faster acceleration out of corner due to NA engine's instant torque

- Keisuke's special training allow him to not run at full throttle but control and make it appear to be running at full throttle

- This allows for better tire management as the FD3S is lighter

General Techniques

- To maximise speed throughout corners, go in slow and exit fast.

- On downhill, car weight and braking skills are more important than horsepower.

- On downhill, the use of curved/angled gutter may shift the center of gravity nearer to the corner, preventing the car from sliding outwards.

- Both tire and brake management are critical, once tire wear out and/or the brakes faded it will be harder to control the car due to lesser grip and/or lesser braking force.

- Tire can be preserved by controling the lines used together with accelerator control and how aggresive the driving is.

- Brake management is controlled mostly by the car weight and partially on how aggresive the driving is.

- On uphill, there is a need to balance horsepower and weight to produce the best power-to-weight ratio.

- The disadvantage of N.A (Naturally Aspirated) engines is the lack of power.

- The disadvantage of turbo-charged engines is the introduction of turbo lag, which means before reaching a certain speed, there would not be much power boost.
